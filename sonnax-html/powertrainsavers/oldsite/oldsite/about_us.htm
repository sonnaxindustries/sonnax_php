About Power Train Savers

Most over-torque damage to trucks goes undetected. This damage can go unnoticed, sometimes for months, as the vehicle is still functioning after over-torque occurs. Most of the time, however, and often within one to four weeks time, the trucks components, which experienced over-torque, will break down at an unexpected time. Nowal Pierson was an owner and operator of his own trucking business who experienced breakdowns on his fleet with excessive downtime and repair costs. He notes that, “when the breakdown occurs the driver at that time has done nothing wrong as the damage took place previously”. Discovering a solution to the expensive repairs, Mr. Pierson designed, created and developed the “Torque Fuse® system”. The Torque Fuses are by far cheaper to replace than differentials, drive shafts, gears, axles, etc. This system protects any drive shaft driven piece of equipment from these costly damages, without the extensive downtime.

Power Train Savers continues to market throughout North America and abroad, with the main focus being the trucking industries. Power Train Savers has also introduced its newest line of product. This line consists of the industrial application flange units. These units mount into a drive system by way of bolting up to input/output flanges and drive shaft companion flanges. PTS flange units are being used on well service pumps in the oil and gas industry, offering complete protection for all of the pumps major and minor components. Other applications for this product include mining, marine, pulp & paper mills and any other industrial applications which are run by a drive shaft system.

Whether your need calls for a weld-in unit for your trucks or a flange unit for industrial purposes, Power Train Savers has a system that can be configured to meet your needs. Power Train Savers work on all systems that operate with torque ranges from 2,000 ft/lbs up to 45,000 ft/lbs.

Power Train Savers is setting the standard in power train over-torque protection.

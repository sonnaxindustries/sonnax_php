PTS NEWS and Info
Drive Shaft With PTS Unit installed

Power Train Savers is a company that manufactures a product that protects trucks and equipment of all sizes from over-torque damage. Our unit gets installed right into your existing drive shaft, and it works on a system of 3 shear pins, which are called Torque Fuses. When over-torque occurs, the 3 Torque Fuses will shear just prior to twisting off the drive shaft or tearing up axles, gears and differentials. After shearing, the unit free wheels on an internal support bushing and pin, which keeps the drive shaft in place rather than falling on the ground and causing other damage. It is then a matter of backing the nuts off, removing the broken Torque Fuse halves, and then replacing them with a new set of Torque Fuses. In about 15 minutes you are up and operating again, eliminating costly downtime and repairs.
Flange Unit
Power Train Savers have also introduced it’s new flange units. These units come with a flange on both ends of the Power Train Saver unit so it will bolt right up to the output flange on power units or the input flange on transmissions, gear boxes, pump power ends, etc. Power Train Savers is currently marketing and selling these units in the industrial and well service pump markets. Companies such as Schlumberger, Trican, Cal Frac and Sanjel are some of the current customers using these units to protect their well service pumps. Power Train Saver units will protect any drive train system with torque ranges from 2,000 ft/lbs up to 45,000 ft/lbs.
3 Torque Fuses
The Power Train Saver unit will last for the life of your trucks and equipment, with the Torque Fuses being the only replacement part needed. The Torque Fuses come in a variety of torque ratings allowing you to customize any set-up.

Identifying Early vs. Late Style 4L60E Pumps

Identifying Early vs. Late Style 4L60E Pumps

Sonnax boost valve kits are available for both early and late style pumps, but how do you know which style pump you have? That's the question one rebuilder came to us with when he was looking for 2001 Chevy 4L60E transmission fixes.

Late pumps began circulation around the 2005 model year, so this transmission would be considered an early design. The difference is that the later pumps provided a provision for an input speed sensor in the stator tube, which necessitated a change in the length of the boost valve sleeve.

It's easy to visually identify pumps of this type. Compared to the relatively smooth surfaces of early style pumps, late style pumps feature a ridge on the casting above a prominently indented bore.
